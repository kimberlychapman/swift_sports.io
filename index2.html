Writing a Function for a Repeating Pattern
The following scenario is used to explain how to write a function for a repeating pattern, and why it is "faster" than writing out every single command.
Students are volunteering to pack lunches: a group of younger students shows up to help; an older student walks over to the group to give them instructions on how to pack the lunches.
The older student leads the group to a table that has a bunch of open lunch bags (in a row) and 3 containers: one holding sandwiches, one holding apples, and one holding cookies.
The older student tells them, "Start here and put one sandwich in the bag, one apple in the bag, and one cookie in the bag."

The older student keeps walking down— along the row of bags, repeating: "one sandwich, one apple, one cookie".
As the older student continues repeating themselves, more empty bags continue to be added to the table, which makes the bags at the opposite end of the table fall off.

While this is happening, the top of the screen fills up with the words "sandwich, apple, cookie" (repeating).
Eventually, the older student stops talking and asks the group of younger students if they have any questions; one of the younger students chimes in and says, "that sounds like a lot of steps...I'm confused."
The older student says to themselves, "there has to be an easier way to explain this". Then their thinking is modeled outloud:

"Okay, so there are a lot of bags, but each bag gets the same things: one sandwich, one apple, and one cookie. So I can just tell them to put a sandwich, an apple, and a cookie in each bag. Then, they can just keep following the pattern until all of the bags are filled."
The older student tells the group of younger students the repeating pattern to follow, and the younger student that spoke before says, "Oh...okay that makes sense."

Finally, as the younger students stand there looking around, another older student "calls" the group over to their table, and tells them, "Come on, start filling the bags".
The pattern is written out in phrases first, and then using swift syntax. Finally, calling a function is explained by referring to the part in the scenario when the group stood there until the other older student called them over and told them to start filling up the bags.
